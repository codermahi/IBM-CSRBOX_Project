Causes of Climate Change

Greenhouse Gas Emissions

Greenhouse gases like carbon dioxide (CO2), methane (CH4), and nitrous oxide (N2O) trap heat in the atmosphere, causing the Earth's temperature to rise. These gases are released from burning fossil fuels, industrial activities, and agricultural practices.

Deforestation

Deforestation involves the large-scale removal of forests, which act as carbon sinks by absorbing CO2 from the atmosphere. When forests are cut down, the stored carbon is released, contributing to greenhouse gas emissions.

Industrial Activities

Industrial activities, including manufacturing and energy production, are major sources of greenhouse gases. Factories and power plants often burn fossil fuels, releasing large amounts of CO2 and other pollutants into the atmosphere.

Agriculture

Agricultural practices, particularly livestock farming, produce methane and nitrous oxide emissions. Additionally, the use of synthetic fertilizers and pesticides contributes to greenhouse gas emissions and environmental degradation.

Fossil Fuel Extraction and Use

The extraction and burning of fossil fuels for energy is the primary source of CO2 emissions. This includes activities such as oil drilling, coal mining, and natural gas extraction, as well as the consumption of these fuels in transportation and electricity generation.
