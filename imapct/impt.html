Overview

Climate change refers to significant changes in global temperatures and weather patterns over time. While climate change is a natural phenomenon, scientific evidence shows that human activities have accelerated its effects.

Effects of Climate Change

Rising Sea Levels

Due to melting ice caps and glaciers, sea levels are rising, posing a threat to coastal communities.

Extreme Weather Events

Increased frequency and severity of hurricanes, droughts, and heatwaves are linked to climate change.

Loss of Biodiversity

Many species are at risk of extinction due to changing habitats and ecosystems.

Solutions to Combat Climate Change

There are several actions we can take to mitigate the effects of climate change, including reducing carbon emissions, adopting renewable energy sources, and protecting natural habitats.
